Google Ads Auction

The Google Ads Auction uses a CPP (Cost Per Click) based model to determine which ads to show.

What factors determine the actual CPC?

  1. Bid Amount: The maximum amount you are willing to pay per click. Competitiveness also plays into the final bid amount.
  2. Quality Score: It is a numeric value on a scale of 1 to 10 that Google assigns to the ad based on the
    • Expected click through rate.
    • Relevance of the ad.
    • Landing Page Experience. (How accurate, navigable, informative the landing page is in relationship to the search in Google.)
  3. Ad Rank: Calculated from the bid score and quality score and the use of Ad formats and extensions.

Setting up bids with Smart Bidding

When using smart bidding strategies, the algorithm sets unique bid individual auctions. It takes the following factors into account:

  1. User Signals & Context: Location, Device, time of the day, day of week, ad schedule, language, operating system.
  2. Behavioral Signals: Previous interactions, remarketing lists, in-market audiences, custom intent audiences.
  3. Historical performance data: Historical performance data of conversion rate and CTR.
  4. Competitive Landscape
  5. Bid Strategy Goals: Target CPA, Target ROAS, maximize conversions, maximize conversion value.
  6. Ad Relevance: Ad relevance and landing page quality/experience.
  7. Daily Budget

The number of advertisers bidding on the same keywords affects CPC. Higher competition can drive up CPC as advertisers bid more to secure top positions.

Understanding Quality Scores

  1. It is a measure of quality and relevance of your ads, keywords, and landing pages.
  2. Influences both your actual CPC and Ad Rank.
  3. Components:
    • Expected click-through rate (CTR).
    • Ad relevance.
    • Landing page experience.

The Ad Rank Formula

  • Higher Ad Rank wins the auction.
  • There are minimum ad rank thresholds to enter into the auction.
  • The use of Ad Extensions (sitelinks, callouts, structured snippets, etc) can improve your ad's performance and quality score, potentially lowering your CPC.

How does the Google Ads Auction Operate?

Max CPC Bid x Quality Score (+ Ad Extensions and Formats)

The Core Structure and Functionality of a Google Ads Account

Diagram illustrating the hierarchical structure of a Google Ads account, including campaigns, ad groups, keywords, ads, and landing pages.

  1. Campaigns: The top level structure where you set goals, budgets and strategy
  2. AD Groups: Subdivisions within campaigns that group related ads and keywords.
  3. Keywords: The terms you target to match your ads with user searches.
  4. Ads: The actual content that users see, including text, images, or videos.
  5. Budgets: The amount you're willing to spend on a campaign per day.
  6. Bids: The acount you are willing to pay for each click or impression.
  7. Targeting: The criteria use to define who sees your ads (e.g., location, demographics, interests).
  8. Extensions: Additional information added to ads, like phone numbers or links to specific pages.
  9. Performance Metrics: Key data points such as clicks, impressions, click-through rate(CTR), and conversions that measure the success of your ads.
  10. Reports: Tools to analyze and visualize your campaign performance.
